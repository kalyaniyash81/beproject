ARIMA Model

ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average) is a statistical model used for time-series forecasting. It is widely used in stock prediction due to its ability to capture trends and seasonality in data.

  • Accuracy: 89.5%
  • RMSE: 12.34
  • MAE: 8.76

LSTM Model

LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) is a type of Recurrent Neural Network (RNN) designed to handle long-term dependencies in sequential data. It is highly effective for stock price prediction due to its ability to learn complex patterns.

  • Accuracy: 95.69%
  • RMSE: 8.45
  • MAE: 6.23

GRU Model

GRU (Gated Recurrent Unit) is a simplified version of LSTM with fewer parameters. It is computationally efficient and performs well in capturing temporal dependencies in stock data.

  • Accuracy: 94.63%
  • RMSE: 9.12
  • MAE: 6.89

Vanilla RNN Model

Vanilla RNN is the simplest form of Recurrent Neural Network. While it struggles with long-term dependencies, it is still useful for short-term stock price predictions.

  • Accuracy: 91.54%
  • RMSE: 10.56
  • MAE: 7.45
